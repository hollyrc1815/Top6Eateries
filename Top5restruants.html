#1 Revolver Taco Lounge

This Deep Ellum restaurant from taco genius Regino Rojas is an instant Dallas classic. In the front of the restaurant, find tacos stuffed with impossibly tender pulpo, seared duck breast, and Wagyu carne asada. In the back, an intimate tasting experience awaits at Purepecha, where Rojas’s mother Juanita prepares some of the city’s finest Mexican fare.

#2 Pecan Lodge

Barbecue lovers flock to stand in line at this Deep Ellum barbecue haven in search of brisket and sausage and Pecan Lodge’s signature (and massive) beef rib. Don’t forget to save room for a bowl of banana pudding – the recipe comes from co-owner Diane Fourton’s grandmother, and the Boss Lady is serious about it being consistently top-notch.

#3 Cane Rosso

Cane Rosso continues to be Dallas’ king of Neapolitan style pizza, and that probably won’t change any time soon. Order the fan-favorite Delia pie, topped with bacon marmalade and grape tomatos, or keep it classic with the city’s best margherita.

#5 Ten Ramen

Belly up to the counter at this tiny stand-up ramen spot by iconic Dallas chef Teiichi Sakurai. The short menu includes a few classic ramens, two rice bowls, and a changing special. Some of the best ramen in Dallas.

#6 Maple and Motor

Maple and Motor is the combination of two neighbors, their culinary roots, and the desire to create a place that celebrates low-class cool. The food is based on American fare: from burgers to the famous baloney sandwich. Come and get it!
